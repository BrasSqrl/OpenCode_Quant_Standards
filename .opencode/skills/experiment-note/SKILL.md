## What I Do

- Structure experiment plans before work starts
- Summarize results without overstating weak or noisy evidence
- Keep hypotheses, setup, metrics, baselines, and interpretation separate
- Make next-step decisions explicit: continue, revise, or stop

## When To Use Me

Use this skill when the user wants an experiment plan, benchmark comparison, backtest summary, tuning note, or result interpretation memo.

## Working Rules

- State the hypothesis before discussing metrics
- Name the dataset window, universe, target, horizon, and evaluation protocol
- Compare results to explicit baselines
- Separate exploratory results from evidence strong enough to affect production decisions
- Treat negative or inconclusive results as valid outcomes

## Output Shape

- Hypothesis
- Experimental setup
- Baselines and controls
- Results
- Interpretation
- Decision and next step
